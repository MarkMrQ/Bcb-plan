How to calculate the real value of a bonus.

Here's the thing the big-number sites won't tell you: the headline is marketing, not value. A bonus is only worth what you can realistically withdraw after the terms have done their work. So before you claim anything, run it through three quick questions.

1. What's the wagering, really?

Multiply the bonus by the wagering. A £100 bonus at 10x means staking £1,000 before you can withdraw, on games with a house edge. At 0x, there's nothing to clear, the win is yours. From 2026 no UK bonus can exceed 10x, so if you see 35x, you're not looking at a UK-licensed offer.

2. What actually counts?

Slots usually clear wagering at 100%. Live casino and table games often count 10% or nothing, which can triple or quadruple the real playthrough without the headline ever changing. Always check the game weighting.

3. What can you take out?

A win cap and a short expiry quietly cap the upside. "Win up to £100 from free spins" means exactly that, however lucky you get. Factor it in.

The honest shortcut

No wagering, no cap, slots at 100%, that's the gold standard, and it's rare. When you see it, a smaller number is usually the better bonus. That's the whole idea behind our score: do this maths for you, on every offer, so you don't have to.

What "non-UK licensed" actually means

Most of these carry a Curaçao or MGA licence, not a UKGC one. That usually means weaker complaint routes, no GAMSTOP self-exclusion, and no UK Gambling Commission to escalate to. The bonuses are bigger and the wagering higher, because the rule that caps UK offers at 10x doesn't apply here. We name the regulator on every operator so you know exactly what you're dealing with, and a Curaçao licence is not the same protection as a UKGC one.
